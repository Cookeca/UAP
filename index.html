Credible Sworn Testimony

Four sworn-under-oath Congressional UAP hearings anchor the modern public record:

  • Apr 19, 2023 — Senate Armed Services. Dr. Sean Kirkpatrick (then AARO Director). Presented Middle East orb imagery including the Mosul Orb (on this map).
  • Jul 26, 2023 — House Oversight. David Grusch (ex-NRO/UAPTF — non-human craft recovery program based on 40+ named-individual interviews); Cmdr. David Fravor (Nimitz 2004 lead pilot); Ryan Graves (Roosevelt VACAPES recurring encounters). The most direct sworn disclosure event in modern US history.
  • Nov 13, 2024 — Joint Senate. Lue Elizondo (ex-AATIP), Tim Gallaudet (RADM, ex-NOAA), Michael Shellenberger, Michael Gold (NASA UAP panel). Focus on classified compartmentalization.
  • Sep 9, 2025 — House Oversight Transparency. Rep. Eric Burlison (R-MO-7) introduced the Yemen-Coast Hellfire Orb footage (on this map).

NDAA UAP Disclosure Act — Three-Year Gutting

The Schumer-Rounds UAP Disclosure Act (UAPDA) — modeled on the JFK Records Act — would have created an independent Review Board, presumptive declassification, subpoena power, and eminent-domain authority over recovered materials. Stripped three NDAAs running:

  • FY2024 (Dec 2023). SA 2610 passed Senate. Conference committee stripped Review Board, eminent domain, subpoena power, and presumptive declassification. What survived: agencies transfer UAP records to NARA with broad national-security exemptions and 25-year postponements.
  • FY2025 (Dec 2024). House version contained no UAP provisions. Conference again excluded substantive Disclosure Act language.
  • FY2026 (pending). Schumer refiled UAPDA Jul 29 2025. Current draft has only a narrower AARO-briefing requirement on USNORTHCOM/NORAD UAP intercepts. Substantive framework again at risk.

HPSCI — And Why The Chair Matters

The House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence (established 1977, post-Church Committee) is the House oversight committee for the entire US Intelligence Community: CIA, NSA, DIA, NRO, NGA, FBI national-security side, military intelligence. UAP disclosure runs through HPSCI because it controls the classified intelligence budget (NIP, ~$70-100B/year), receives all SCI briefings on SAPs and USAPs (the compartments whistleblowers allege UAP material is held in), drafts the Intelligence Authorization Act, runs IC whistleblower intake (Grusch's 2022 protected disclosure went through HPSCI), and gatekeeps whether classified UAP material reaches the National Archives.

The Chair has unilateral authority over hearing schedules, witness subpoenas, staff clearances, classified briefings, NDAA conference positions, and whistleblower advancement. Functionally a single-person veto on the entire House classified-disclosure pipeline. This is why Mike Turner's chairmanship (Jan 2023 – Jan 2025) was disproportionately consequential — a one-person bottleneck during the most active UAP-disclosure period in modern Congressional history.

Current Chair: Rep. Rick Crawford (R-AR-1), appointed Jan 16, 2025 by Speaker Johnson — per Rep. Burlison, more disclosure-friendly than Turner. Ranking Member: Rep. Jim Himes (D-CT-4). Turner retained his committee seat after losing the chair.

HPSCI seats are among the most coveted in the House — Speakers historically reserve them for members from defense-economy or intelligence-economy districts. The pattern self-reinforces: members chosen for constituent defense interests then make disclosure decisions affecting those same interests.

Pattern Analysis — What 128 Cases Show

Reading note — selection bias is real

This map is biased toward cases where investigation actually happened, witnesses were credible, and records survived. Every pattern below is filtered through that lens. Findings are flagged where they likely represent real signal vs. where they are more likely artifacts of who reports and who investigates.

1. The map is a snapshot of state capacity, not phenomenon density

Six countries account for ~60% of cases: United States, Russia/USSR, Brazil, France, Chile/Argentina, and the UK. These are exactly the countries with active or historical formal government UAP investigation programs. The 130+ countries with no bar-clearing case are not places where UAP don't happen — they're places without state capacity to investigate or preserve records. The correlation between national-program existence and case-on-map probability is near 1.0.

If you want the map to look different, the lever isn't more witnesses — it's more institutional investigation.

2. Witness class — military observers dominate

Of 91 single-event cases: ~45 named military pilot or radar operator, ~15 commercial airline pilot, ~10 police / civilian-government employee, ~20 civilian-only. Cases without a trained observer almost never clear the bar regardless of how dramatic they are. We are systematically blind to whatever phenomenon, if any, occurs in spaces civilians frequent but trained observers don't.

3. Temporal patterns

Decadal density looks roughly stable from the 1950s, with upticks in the 1970s-80s and 2010s-2020s — misleading: older cases have had decades to surface via declassification, recent cases are still being released. True distribution is likely flatter.

~75% of timed cases occur dusk-to-dawn. Partly real (luminous objects more visible at night, air defense exercises run at night), partly artifact (atmospheric optical phenomena are also more dramatic at night).

Wave peaks tend to start late summer / autumn (Belgian wave Nov 1989, Hudson Valley peaks spring/summer, Petrozavodsk Sept 1977). Simplest explanation: summer-night sky-watching exposure + atmospheric inversion conditions, not phenomenon seasonality.

4. The nuclear correlation is real and persistent

One of the strongest patterns in the dataset. Cases with explicit nuclear involvement:

  • Malmstrom AFB 1967 — 10 Minuteman ICBMs offline coincident with UAP
  • Iran nuclear facility cluster 2004-2012 — F-14 intercepts at Bushehr, Arak, Natanz
  • Usove (Ukraine) 1982 — UAP-coincident missile-launch-control activation
  • Jaslovské Bohunice 2005 — Slovak nuclear plant incursion + AF response
  • Russell/Baku 1955 — Senator + CIA OSI during Soviet nuclear weapons development
  • Lake Baikal — Soviet Northern Fleet nuclear submarine operations
  • Bikini/Marshall Islands 1946-58 — nuclear test era reports

Correlation repeats across adversarial nations (US + USSR + Iran + Slovakia + UK) and multiple decades. Skeptical reading: nuclear sites have the densest air defense radar, most trained observers, most aggressive interception doctrine — they generate more documentation regardless of phenomenon. Non-skeptical reading: too clean across adversarial systems for pure observer bias. Honest assessment: correlation is real; causal interpretation contested. Selection bias carries ~70%; the residual 30% is what makes investigators take this pattern seriously.

5. Geography — five terrain types account for nearly all hotspots

  • Active volcanoes — Popocatépetl, Mt Etna. Webcam infrastructure does the selection.
  • Open ocean Navy operating areas — SoCal Op Area, Bass Strait, VACAPES (Graves), Yemen coast, Aguadilla. Sensor-dense by design.
  • High-altitude deserts with extreme dark skies — San Luis Valley, Marfa, Tatacoa, Salar de Uyuni, Zona del Silencio. Maximizes visibility of routine + anomalous.
  • Mountain ranges with pre-Columbian / indigenous sacred status — Capilla del Monte, Mt Shasta, Sedona, Tepoztlán, Peña de Juaica, Hessdalen, Kongka La. Overlap is striking. Probably reflects (a) inherited cultural attention producing sustained witness pools, and (b) the human tendency to mark out specific peaks as sacred is itself partly driven by atmospheric optical features (lenticular clouds, electrostatic discharge) that pre-modern populations interpreted spiritually.
  • Strategic military airspace — Bahía Blanca, Bulawayo, Kayseri-Erkilet AFB, Yakima Reservation (downwind of YTC), nuclear-site cases.

The map has very few cases over genuinely uninhabited terrain — open ocean away from Navy Op Areas, Sahara interior, Tibetan plateau, deep Amazon. Almost certainly observer-density artifact, not phenomenon absence.

6. Morphology — six recurring shapes across 90 years

  • Disc / saucer — dominant pre-1990s (Trans-en-Provence, Father Gill, Westall, Cangzhou, Tully)
  • Cigar / tic-tac / oblong — Nimitz 2004, JAL 1628, Mozambique DETA, Bulawayo 1985, Iran 2012, Bass Strait, Marfa
  • Triangle / boomerang / V-shape — Hudson Valley, Belgian wave, Phoenix Lights, Stephenville, Bonnybridge
  • Orb / sphere — Mosul Orb, Yemen Hellfire, Genkai, Aguadilla, Yakima, Min Min, Brown Mountain, Hessdalen typical
  • Egg / dome — Knowles 1988, Chu Lai 1969, Capilla del Monte, Trans-en-Provence
  • Complex / domed-disc with windows or lights — Father Gill, Lemos Ferreira, Iino-machi reports

The two morphologies that have gained share in the past 20 years are orbs and tic-tacs. Disc reports have declined since the 1990s. Consistent with the hypothesis that morphology reporting is partly culturally shaped — disc reports peaked when "flying saucer" was the dominant cultural frame, and have declined as the Pentagon Tic Tac + orb releases shifted the template.

7. Behavioral patterns

Recurring observed behaviors, in rough order of robustness:

  • Pacing aircraft — JAL 1628 (50+ min over 400 km), Mozambique DETA, Bulawayo, Tehran 1976, Aer Lingus EI-DAA, RB-47 (across multiple US states), Manises 1979. Unusually consistent across cultures and decades.
  • Sudden high-G maneuvering / right-angle reversals — Nimitz (Mach 5+ in seconds), Belgian wave (40+G), JAL 1628, Chile Navy 2014. Confirmed in radar logs.
  • Reaction to radar lock — Belgian F-16 wave 1989-90: APG-66 lock triggered evasion; lock-break resumed normal behavior. Differential sensor-state response implies awareness of being targeted.
  • Withdrawal when actively pursued — Tehran F-4 1976, Lakenheath 1956 RAF Venom pursuit. Position held when observed, retreat when intercepted.
  • Selective visual avoidance — Nimitz "cloaking," Andersen Guam "always in cloud." Permits radar/IR detection while avoiding clean visual.
  • Sensor / electrical interference — Trans-en-Provence vegetation, Iran 2012 F-14 instrument failures, Kuwait 1978 KOC shutdown.
  • Air-to-water transitions — Aguadilla, Lake Baikal USOs, Bass Strait. Cross-cultural but thinner sensor backing.

Pacing is engagement. An object capable of hypersonic flight that chooses to fly alongside a specific aircraft is making a deliberate choice. The pacing-plus-radar-lock-response pattern is the strongest argument against natural-phenomenon explanations: ball lightning does not track an F/A-18, and plasma vortices do not get behind a chasing Venom and match its turns. Intelligence control is required.

What never appears: linguistic dialogue, response to deliberate human signaling, repeatable on-demand summoning (Bledsoe's claim aside as a testimony outlier — Elizondo described passive orb recurrence at his home, not summoning), sustained instrumented ground interactions, return-on-schedule, or any coherent contact agenda. The accurate framing: engages behaviorally but not communicatively. It watches, paces, reacts, withdraws — in the form a surveiller would use, not a contactor.

8. Resolution rates

Of 91 single-event cases: ~12 formally resolved mundane (Campeche/Greifswald flares, Minsk SLBM, Mogul alloy, Aztec/Kalahari hoaxes, Aguadilla, Mosul Orb partial), ~3 documented disinfo/hoax, ~25 contested, ~25 testimony-strong without physical evidence, ~25-30 multi-sensor anomalous unresolved (Nimitz, Belgian, JAL 1628, Manises, Roosevelt FLIR, Cangzhou).

Headline: ~one-third of bar-clearing cases remain unresolved-anomalous despite decades of investigation. Not the same as "one-third are real" — AARO has resolved cases that previous eras left open, suggesting the unresolved fraction would shrink with more investigation. The cases least likely to ever resolve: Nimitz 2004, Belgian wave 1989-90, JAL 1628 1986 — prosaic hypotheses have been tested most rigorously and consistently failed to account for the full record.

9. Patterns that fail to appear

No fine-scale population correlation. Within countries, cases cluster on military presence, dark skies, atmospheric conditions, and reporting infrastructure — not population. The "happens near people" intuition is a world-map projection artifact.

No clear weather correlation. Cases appear across all weather including overcast and rain (Bulawayo 1985, Hessdalen, Hudson Valley).

No sharp pre-1947 → post-1947 step function. Min Min (1918), Ghost Rockets (1946), Brown Mountain (1771), Marfa (1883) sit in the same phenomenological space as post-1947 cases. The "modern UFO era" is media history, not phenomenon history.

10. Hotspots and bar-clearing cases are geographically disjoint

The 32 hotspots (pink ring pins) and the strongest individual cases live in almost completely different places. Stephenville TX and Hudson Valley NY are the only meaningful crossovers. This is structural, not noise.

What hotspots share: remote/rural/low-population (Marfa, Wycliffe Well, Yakima, Hessdalen, Iino-machi, Brown Mountain, Min Min, San Luis Valley); dark skies; an on-site chronicler collecting reports (Akers/Yakima, Strand/Hessdalen, Bunnell/Marfa — probably the single biggest factor); pre-existing folklore (Aboriginal Dreamtime, Cherokee, Appalachian haint, Sami/Norse); often a tourism economy that monetizes the reputation; sometimes a real atmospheric mechanism (Hessdalen scheelite+radon plasma, Min Min/Brown Mountain Fata Morgana, Marfa headlight tracking). Hotspot status does not require a real anomalous phenomenon — recurring optical artifact + chronicler is sufficient.

Where bar-clearing cases live instead: military exercise areas with multi-sensor platforms (Nimitz, Roosevelt, RB-47, Lakenheath, Andersen Guam); commercial aviation corridors with ATC radar (JAL 1628, Aer Lingus, TACA 510); nuclear infrastructure with cleared observers (Malmstrom, SRF sites); foreign-government investigation regimes (CEFAA, GEPAN/COMETA, Belgian F-16, Iran F-4 1976).

The two filters select for opposite geographies — rural+dark+chronicler versus militarized+sensor-dense+declassifiable. Hotspots filter for repeat witnessing; bar-clearing cases filter for instrumented sensing.

Two readings, both fit: A — phenomenon is uniform, hotspots are mundane misidentifications by attentive locals, bar-clearing cases occur wherever a real anomaly hits a sensor envelope; disjointness is selection bias. B — phenomenon clusters near strategic infrastructure (bar-clearing = signal), hotspots are mostly noise produced by geology+culture+chronicler effects.

Confidence on the disjointness itself: high. Confidence on which reading is correct: low. The honest move is to treat hotspots and bar-clearing cases as different evidentiary classes — they probably are not measuring the same thing.

11. Hotspots cluster on pre-modern sites of recurring sky-light tradition

Cross-checking all 32 hotspots against pre-modern oral or textual traditions describing sky phenomena at the same location: ~47% have substantial pre-colonial Indigenous traditions tied to the site, 75% have at least some traditional cultural context, only 25% are predominantly modern. This is a stronger predictor of hotspot status than geology, nuclear proximity, or any other physical factor.

Tier 1 (explicit pre-modern tradition for sky-light phenomena at the exact site): Min Min (Aboriginal Dreamtime), Brown Mountain (Cherokee narrative of lost warriors), San Luis Valley (Navajo "sky katchinas," 12-tribe vision-quest area), Kongka La (Tibetan + Sanskrit vimana texts), Mt Etna (Greek Hephaestus, 2,500+ yr record), Popocatépetl (Aztec warrior Popoca, pre-Hispanic codices). Six.

Tier 2 (pre-colonial sacred site, less directly tied to lights): Wycliffe Well, Yakama, Skinwalker Ranch, Cerro Uritorco, Peña de Juaica, Lake Baikal, Tepoztlán, Tully, Montserrat. Nine.

Tier 3 (generic or post-colonial overlay): Hudson Valley, Chestnut Ridge, Marfa, Iino-machi, Bass Strait, Bolsón de Mapimí, Salar de Uyuni, Tatacoa, Malargüe. Nine.

Tier 4 (modern, no deep tradition): Hessdalen, Bonnybridge, Karamay, Alto Paraíso, Vale do Paraíba, Bahía Blanca, Molebka, SoCal Pacific. Eight — exactly the ones that needed a modern researcher (Strand at Hessdalen) to substitute for cultural tradition.

Three confounds block the obvious "phenomenon has been observed continuously for centuries" reading: confirmation bias (Indigenous traditions are rich enough to find "sky beings" anywhere); researcher narrative bias (the "Indigenous people knew first" trope is heavily publication-selected); reverse causation (oral traditions enable modern reporting, not phenomenon density — strongest competing explanation).

Confidence: tradition-hotspot overlap is real — high. Tradition outpredicts physical factors — moderate-to-high. Real continuous phenomenon vs cultural-framework-enabling-reports — undetermined.

This sharpens the §10 Reading A/B framing without resolving it. Reading A gets support because Tier 4 hotspots needed a modern chronicler-substitute. Reading B gets support because six unrelated traditions converged on the same sites — not trivial. Either way: whatever hotspots are, they are not a 20th-century phenomenon, and any complete explanation must account for the cross-cultural depth.

12. The classified-sensor blackout

The most sensor-rich military installations on Earth produce almost no public UAP record. The absence is itself a data point.

Sites with extreme sensor density and near-zero public UAP record: Kwajalein/Reagan Test Site (ALTAIR/TRADEX/MMW/ALCOR), Diego Garcia, Pine Gap, Menwith Hill, Ascension Island, PAVE PAWS sites (Beale/Cape Cod/Clear), BMEWS sites (Pituffik/Fylingdales/Clear — post-1961 BMEWS-era record essentially blank), NTTR (Tonopah/Groom Lake/Nellis), Eglin/Gulf Range.

The strong cases on this map come from the periphery: commercial pilots and ATC (JAL 1628, TACA 510, Aer Lingus), Blue Book-era loose compartmentalization (RB-47, Lakenheath, Andersen Guam), low-classification ranges (Nimitz, Roosevelt, Aguadilla), foreign-government regimes with different declassification rules (CEFAA, GEPAN/COMETA, Kohanudnas).

Four readings:

  1. Genuine absence. Unlikely — nearby less-classified sites do see UAP. Confidence: low.
  2. Classification compartmentalization. SAR/SAP data has no declassification pathway analogous to Blue Book or AARO. Confidence: high.
  3. Signature filtering. Radars tuned for specific signatures discard out-of-envelope returns at operator level as artifacts. Confidence: moderate.
  4. Active suppression. Per Grusch/Elizondo. Unfalsifiable from open record; consistent with the pattern. Confidence: cannot assess.

Takeaway: the unclassified record is built from the periphery, not the center. What looks like a hot region (VACAPES, Hessdalen, Brazilian Amazon) may be hot partly because it is one of the few high-sensor zones where data reaches the public domain.

13. The crash-retrieval asymmetry

The strongest cases on this map are encounters, not crash retrievals. Among strength-4-5 anomalous cases, only one involves a crash with claimed material recovery — and it has serious skeptical challenges.

Strength-4-5 crash + retrieval inventory:

  • Dalnegorsk 1986 (4/anomalous) — the only one. Soviet Academy of Sciences recovered lead spherules, rare-earth mesh, ultra-pure molybdenum, shape-memory quartz fiber. But lead isotope analysis matched the Kholodchensky terrestrial deposit (Pribaikalye). RAS Pseudoscience Commission considers it closed (Soviet reconnaissance balloon + thermite charge).
  • Roswell 1947 (5/contested) — AARO Vol I (2024) endorses Project Mogul. Disputed at the material-analysis level: Jesse Marcel Jr. memory-metal accounts predate nitinol's 1962 patent; Battelle/Wright-Patterson contracting timeline cited by disclosure researchers; Nolan/Vallée 2022 peer-reviewed work raised questions about UAP-attributed material composition (though not specifically Roswell). No Roswell debris with public chain of custody has been published in neutral peer review.
  • Mogul Alloy 2024 (4/resolved) — AARO + lab analysis: magnesium-zinc-bismuth alloy, terrestrial.
  • Aztec 1948 (4/disinfo), Kalahari 1989 (4/disinfo) — confirmed hoaxes.
  • El Taire 1978 (4/contested) — alleged US Project Moon Dust recovery; no primary docs surfaced.
  • Shag Harbour 1967 (4/anomalous) — splashdown into Nova Scotia harbour; RCN+RCMP search inconclusive; no retrieval.
  • Cuban MiG-21 1967 (4/anomalous) — the MiG crashed during UAP intercept, not the UAP.
  • Grusch 2023 (4/testimony) — sworn claims of multi-decade recovery programs; no specific verifiable crash event named.

What dominates the strength-5 anomalous tier instead: Nimitz, Belgian wave, Tehran F-4, JAL 1628, RB-47, Lakenheath, Aguadilla, Andersen Guam, Aer Lingus, Trindade, Tananarive — every one is an observation of an object that did not come down.

Public UAP discourse fixates on crash retrieval (Roswell, Grusch, Moment of Contact, Aztec, Kingman, Trinity NM). The strength-4-5 record contains almost none. Structural mismatch between public expectation and bar-clearing reality.

Three readings, none distinguishable from current data: A — the phenomenon doesn't crash (intelligence-controlled, high reliability). B — phenomenon is mundane / not unified; mundane causes don't leave retrievable wreckage; fits the data well. C — crashes happen but recoveries are classified (Grusch); consistent with §12 blackout; unfalsifiable from open record.

Whatever the phenomenon is, it is observed in flight, not recovered from the ground.

14. Biggest country-level gap

East Asia (excl. China) is dramatically underrepresented. South Korea: Seoul 1976. Japan: JAL 1628, JASDF Sato, Iino-machi, Genkai 2025. Taiwan: 1996 Matsu/Dongyin bar-near miss. That's it for a region with ~200M people, substantial military aviation, and dense Cold War air defense networks.

Likely causes: (a) East Asian UFO research is in JA/KO/ZH sources hard to penetrate from English search; (b) cultural norms produce less Western-style declassification; (c) China's info-control on Xinjiang and Tibet blocks reports. East Asia is where the highest signal-per-search-effort would likely come from in any future round.

15. The meta-pattern

  1. "UAP" is not one thing. Aggregates atmospheric optics (Marfa, Brown Mountain, Min Min), space launch / reentry (Minsk, Khartoum Zuma), classified aerospace (Iran), balloons (Aguadilla, possibly Mosul), and a multi-sensor residual.
  2. The residual is small but stubborn. ~25-30 cases. Clusters in time around nuclear infrastructure, in space around military exercise areas, in morphology around tic-tac / orb / fast-mover. Not random.
  3. Engages behaviorally but not communicatively. Pacing, radar-lock response, withdrawal-when-pursued — awareness is required. No linguistic dialogue, no signaling response, no on-demand summoning, no coherent contact agenda. Surveiller behavior, not contactor behavior. Consistent with classified terrestrial aerospace, NHI in surveillance posture, or multiple distinct things — not consistent with natural atmospheric/plasma phenomena.
  4. Institutional dynamics matter as much as the data. The strongest cases come from adversarial-military countries, with records selectively classified for political reasons. We see the phenomenon through the filter of national security policy.
  5. The calibration cases are the most underrated. Minsk 1984 (SLBM), Kalahari 1989 (hoax), Campeche 2004 (oil flares), Greifswald 1990 (SAB flares) show what a "textbook UFO case" looks like fully investigated. Anyone serious should weight these as heavily as the dramatic unresolved ones.

The map is a snapshot of 80 years of selective investigation by selective states with selective records — not a snapshot of the phenomenon. Whether the unresolved fraction shrinks toward zero as investigation continues, or stabilizes at an irreducible core, is the question AARO and successors are trying to answer.

Potential Conclusions — Three Populations, Not One

"UAP" is a label, not a phenomenon. The cases fall into three populations with different witness profiles, sensor signatures, and candidate explanations. Conclusions depend on separating them first. Lumping them together is the most common analytical mistake in the field.

Craft

Variable size — tic-tacs, triangles, discs, sometimes much larger. Multi-sensor verified (radar + IR + visual). Sustained hypersonic performance, near-instantaneous direction changes. Trans-medium movement — observed entering and exiting water at high speed without apparent deceleration, splash, or structural failure (Aguadilla 2013 DHS thermal, Lake Baikal Russian Navy reports since 1958, SoCal Op Area submerged tic-tac contacts, Bass Strait alleged sea entries). Pace military aircraft, react differentially to radar lock, withdraw when actively pursued. These are the cases that anchor the bar-clearing core of the map.

Examples: Nimitz 2004, Belgian wave 1989-90, Tehran F-4 1976, JAL 1628, RB-47 1957, Lakenheath/Bentwaters 1956, Aer Lingus EI-DAA 2018, Andersen Guam 1966, Roosevelt VACAPES 2014-15, Trindade 1958, Chile Navy 2014.

Likely composition, most to least:

  • Classified US aerospace — most likely the largest share. The US has by far the most mature SAP/USAP infrastructure for hiding programs. The Bennewitz case documents AFOSI deliberately using UAP framing as cover for classified US programs. Most strong cases occur in US-controlled or US-aligned airspace. The Belgian wave's timing matches B-2 first flight. The F-117 development cycle (1978-83) produced delta-wing-shape reports across the southwestern US. US R&D outspends Russia and China combined. AFOSI / classified program protection is the most institutionally precedented explanation.
  • Classified adversary aerospace — smaller share. Russian and Chinese hypersonic platforms, low-observable systems in development. The Iranian F-4 1976 case fits Soviet interest in US reconnaissance assets over Iran. Modern hypersonics (DF-ZF, Avangard) plausibly explain some recent encounters. Less plausible for Cold War cases when adversary tech was insufficient. The 2023 Chinese balloon over Montana reframed how much adversary intrusion the US tolerates.
  • Natural atmospheric or plasma phenomenon — smaller still. Substantially downgraded by the pacing pattern. Natural phenomena do not selectively track aircraft and respond to radar lock-on. Ball lightning doesn't get behind a chasing Venom. Explains some subset where the "object" doesn't actually maneuver intelligently.
  • Genuine residual — small but stubborn. Cases that don't reduce cleanly to any of the above. This is the analytically interesting set. Whatever it is, it's observed in flight, surveils military activity, doesn't crash, doesn't communicate, doesn't arrive on demand.

The pacing-and-radar-lock-response pattern requires intelligence control. The question is which intelligence. The institutional opacity around the topic (classified-sensor blackout, UAPDA gutting, AARO mandate hollowing) is more consistent with protecting classified US programs than with adversary intrusion or NHI — but it doesn't rule the others out.

How could humans build something like this?

The Nimitz tic-tac and similar cases describe behavior that breaks known physical limits in four independent ways:

  • 40-100G maneuvering exceeds material strength. Aerospace composites yield at 20-30G; structural metals at similar levels. No public material survives sustained 40G+ in an aircraft-sized object.
  • Biological occupants do not survive 40G+ under conventional force. Humans lose consciousness at ~9G sustained, peak survival ~15-20G briefly. 40G+ kills a human pilot instantly.
  • Sustained hypersonic acceleration with no exhaust implies energy density beyond any public propulsion system. Chemical, nuclear thermal, and ion propulsion all leave signatures. The Nimitz tic-tac left none.
  • Trans-medium movement is independently impossible under conventional engineering. Water is roughly 830× denser than air. No platform optimized for hypersonic atmospheric flight survives water entry at speed.

These are not engineering problems waiting for stronger materials or better fuel. They are physical limits under known mechanics. If the performance data is real, the craft cannot be operating under publicly-known physics.

The simplest physics-coherent explanation is some form of field manipulation — gravity, inertial mass, or local-spacetime control. A craft that manipulates its own inertial field experiences no g-forces internally regardless of external acceleration. The same field, treated as a medium-displacement mechanism, makes air and water equivalent — both are simply medium to be moved around the craft rather than pushed through. One underlying capability resolves the 40G constraint, the biological-survival constraint, the energy-deficit constraint (no reaction mass, no exhaust), and the trans-medium constraint simultaneously. Four anomalies collapse into one physics requirement.

Could humans have discovered this and concealed it? In principle, yes — and the institutional system was built specifically for this scenario. The Atomic Energy Act of 1946 (amended 1954) was created because the US had been caught off guard by the nuclear-physics breakthrough. Atomic theory had been independently developed across multiple countries by physicists who were not government employees. The US could not legally prevent foreign or domestic scientists from rediscovering and publishing what the Manhattan Project had hidden. The Soviets matched the bomb within four years (1949) partly through espionage but partly through independent capability. Congress's institutional answer was to create a legal category — "Restricted Data" — that automatically classifies covered information at the moment of creation, before any government employee ever sees it, applying even to civilian scientists who independently derive it. The doctrine is forward-looking by design: it exists so that the next revolutionary physics breakthrough cannot escape government control before policy catches up. Court-tested in US v. Progressive (1979). National laboratories (Los Alamos, Sandia, Lawrence Livermore, Oak Ridge, Idaho National Lab) hold Q-clearance researchers conducting born-classified work the public cannot evaluate. Public-key cryptography sat classified at GCHQ for 25 years before Diffie-Hellman and RSA independently rediscovered it — a demonstration that the system can in fact hold breakthroughs for decades. Bob Lazar's 1989 claims about a "gravity-wave amplifier" architecture at S-4 describe exactly the kind of work born-classified doctrine would protect — his credentials are mostly unverified, but the structure of the claim fits.

But the duration is unprecedented if true. Manhattan was concealed three years. Public-key crypto twenty-five. The NRO's existence thirty-one (and only its existence, not its capabilities). 60-80 years of complete operational secrecy for a working gravity-manipulation program has no historical precedent. The longer concealment runs, the more people read in, the more contractor relationships accumulate, the more documentary trail exists. The Manhattan analogue establishes that humans can hide breakthrough physics. It does not establish that humans can hide it for eighty years.

The honest read. The data forces a hard choice. If the Nimitz-class performance numbers are real, the craft operates under physics not in the public catalog. That physics has either been discovered by humans and concealed (precedented mechanism, unprecedented duration) or has not been discovered by humans — which means someone else discovered it. The third option — that the multi-sensor + visual + radar convergence is misreading the actual performance — requires explaining away the convergence itself. No clean exit exists. Whichever direction you pick, you are claiming something significant about physics, secrecy, or the reliability of military sensors.

Orbs

Small, self-luminous, slow-moving or hovering. Appear at residences, ranches, sacred sites. Typically 1-5 witnesses, rarely instrumented. Often religiously or mystically framed by witnesses.

Examples: Bledsoe Cape Fear, Elizondo's home (passive recurrence, not summoned), Skinwalker Ranch, Brown Mountain, Marfa, Hessdalen, Min Min, Yakima Reservation.

Likely composition, most to least:

  • Small classified ISR or SOF drones — especially residential orbs near military infrastructure. Bledsoe's property is adjacent to Fort Bragg (JSOC, USASOC, 75th Rangers — highest US special-operations density on earth). Elizondo's experiences began after his AATIP work. Bingham's "summoning" produces what's identifiable as drones. Less plausible pre-2000.
  • Atmospheric plasma and ionization — earth-light hypothesis (Hessdalen scheelite + radon), Fata Morgana (Min Min), ball lightning, temperature-inversion mirages.
  • Misidentified conventional objects — Venus, satellites, ISS passes, Iridium flares, distant aircraft, fireballs, drones — primed by local tradition or expectation.
  • Cultural-experiential framing — for the contactee subset (Bledsoe, ECETI, etc.), shared experiential template within a tight community is a real candidate.
  • Small residual genuinely unidentified — possibly distinct from Craft, possibly related.

Cross-cultural pre-modern oral traditions describe similar phenomena at many of these locations (Pattern Analysis section 11). Whether that reflects a continuous real phenomenon or a continuous cultural framework that enables reporting is undetermined.

Other

Real physics, misclassified as anomalous when investigation is incomplete. Atmospheric optics, plasma, sensor artifacts, conventional platforms identified after rigorous analysis. The largest category by far across all UAP reports — and a meaningful fraction of even the bar-clearing map.

Resolution paths:

  • Atmospheric mirages and inversions — Min Min (Fata Morgana via Pettigrew), Brown Mountain (temperature inversion), Marfa (Highway 67 headlights, partially demonstrated)
  • Space launch / reentry events — Minsk 1984 (SLBM), Khartoum 2018 (SpaceX Zuma), Brazilian "Official Night" 1986 (partial)
  • Military flares and test munitions — Greifswald 1990 (SAB target flares), Phoenix Lights 1997 (second-phase A-10 flares)
  • Industrial / commercial signatures — Campeche FAM 2004 (Cantarell oil platform flares)
  • Confirmed hoaxes — Kalahari 1989 (van Greunen confession), Aztec 1948 (Scully), Lazar (no credible verification)
  • Reconnaissance balloons — Mosul Orb 2016 (AARO), 2023 Chinese balloon reframed the category entirely
  • Misanalyzed material — Mogul Alloy 2024 (AARO determined terrestrial magnesium-zinc-bismuth alloy)
  • Sensor artifacts — FLIR gimbal rotation (the "Gimbal" video's spin is the camera's gimbal, not the object), radar anomalous propagation under inversion conditions

The calibration cases on the map (Minsk, Kalahari, Campeche, Greifswald, Mogul Alloy) explicitly show what a "textbook UFO case" looks like when fully investigated. Anyone serious about the phenomenon should weight these heavily — they tell you what kinds of strong cases turn out to be when somebody does the work.

Where the categories blur

The categories are not clean. Borderline cases worth flagging:

  • Aguadilla 2013 — orb morphology but Craft-tier performance (air-to-water transition, apparent splitting). The map treats it as Craft because of DHS sensor data.
  • Hessdalen — orb appearance with instrumented research suggesting plasma mechanism. Could be Other that became Orb through chronicler effect (Project Hessdalen since 1983).
  • WWII "foo fighters" — orbs that paced military aircraft. Orb shape with Craft behavior. Either A-population objects with B-population behavior, or natural plasma effects miscategorized.
  • Skinwalker Ranch — multi-modal: orbs, "doorways," instrument malfunction, animal effects. Fits no single category cleanly. May indicate categories are aspects of one phenomenon, or that the Ranch is contaminated by hoaxing and confirmation bias across decades.

Some real overlap exists. Whether it reflects fundamentally different phenomena with occasionally overlapping behaviors, or different aspects of one underlying thing, is undetermined.

Bottom line

Most UAP reports are Other. Take that as the baseline. The calibration cases anchor what the answer looks like when investigation is done properly.

Craft are real and contain intelligence-controlled platforms. The pacing pattern requires this. Most likely a US-dominant classified terrestrial mix, with a smaller adversary share, a smaller still natural-phenomenon share, and a small residual that fits none of those.

Orbs are a real recurring category but probably not what the disclosure community claims. The mix includes small classified drones, atmospheric effects, misidentifications, and cultural-experiential framing more than it includes literal non-human contact tools.

If anything is genuinely non-human, it sits in the small Craft residual. Observed in flight, surveilling military activity, not crashing, not communicating, not arriving on demand. Not the Roswell crash narrative. Not the Bledsoe contactee narrative. Something smaller and stranger than either.

Anyone asserting more certainty than this — in either direction — is overclaiming.

Evidence quality tiers

Pins are tiered by the strength of the documentary record. The number tells you how much you can trust whatever conclusion the case reaches.

1 — Resolved (mundane): investigation produced a confident conventional explanation (balloon, flare, reentry, optical effect). High evidence quality; conclusion is "not anomalous."

1 — Hoax / fabrication: confessed or otherwise documented as deliberate deception. Same tier as Resolved because both are "well-investigated, no anomaly remains."

2 — Anecdotal / experiencer: multi-witness or investigated, but no sensor data and limited corroboration. Includes abductions, missing-time, contactee accounts (Betty & Barney Hill, Knowles Nullarbor, Walton, Pascagoula).

3 — Contested: real evidence exists but a credible conventional explanation also fits the data. Could go either way (Stephenville, Iran nuclear cluster, Mosul Orb, El Taire).

4 — Strong witness testimony: credible named witnesses, often military or government investigation, but no instrumented sensor data (Tehran F-4 1976, Father Gill, Grusch testimony).

5 — Multi-sensor confirmed: radar + IR + visual convergence; military or commercial pilot witnesses; government investigation. The strongest evidentiary tier (Nimitz, Belgian wave, Aer Lingus, Andersen Guam, RB-47, Lakenheath).

⊛ Recurring hotspot: location with 3+ events recurring over years in ~25 km — orthogonal marker, not an evidence tier.

☄ Space-based: orbital, cislunar, or lunar observation — orthogonal marker.
