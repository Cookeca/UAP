Credible Sworn Testimony

The current public evidentiary record rests heavily on a small number of sworn-under-oath Congressional hearings. The relevant events:

April 19, 2023 — Senate Armed Services Subcommittee on Emerging Threats

Dr. Sean Kirkpatrick (then AARO Director) gave the first sustained Senate testimony on the standing US UAP investigation. Presented metallic-orb imagery from Middle East operations (including the Mosul Orb that is on this map) as representative of unresolved cases.

July 26, 2023 — House Oversight UAP Hearing

Three named witnesses under oath: David Grusch (former NRO senior officer + UAPTF member, testified that the US operates a non-human craft recovery and reverse-engineering program based on his interviews with 40+ named individuals); Cmdr. David Fravor (lead F/A-18 pilot, USS Nimitz 2004 Tic Tac); Ryan Graves (former US Navy F/A-18 pilot, recurring Atlantic UAP encounters). This is the most direct sworn-witness disclosure event in modern US history.

November 13, 2024 — Joint Senate UAP Hearing

Bipartisan Senate Subcommittee hearing including Lue Elizondo (former AATIP), Tim Gallaudet (retired Rear Admiral, former NOAA chief), Michael Shellenberger (investigative journalist), Michael Gold (former NASA UAP study panel). Focus on classified compartmentalized programs and information access.

September 9, 2025 — House Oversight UAP Transparency Hearing

Sworn witnesses incl. current/former service members. Rep. Eric Burlison (R-MO-7) introduced the Yemen-Coast Hellfire Orb footage (on this map). Investigative-journalist + government-oversight-attorney panels.

Each of the above is bar-clearing testimony in the sense relevant to this map — sworn, named, on-the-record. Their credibility differs by witness but the institutional framing is established.

NDAA UAP Disclosure Act — Three-Year Gutting

The Schumer-Rounds UAP Disclosure Act (UAPDA) was modeled on the JFK Records Act and would have created an independent Review Board, presumptive declassification of UAP records, subpoena power, and eminent-domain authority over recovered materials. It has been substantively stripped three NDAAs running.

FY2024 NDAA (late 2023) — the original gutting

Schumer-Rounds amendment SA 2610 passed the Senate Dec 7 2023. In House-Senate conference committee, the substantive elements were stripped: independent Review Board, eminent domain, subpoena power, and presumptive declassification all removed. What survived: a requirement that federal agencies transfer UAP records to the National Archives, with disclosure subject to broad national-security exemptions and 25-year postponement periods.

FY2025 NDAA (late 2024) — second gutting

Sen. Rounds reintroduced the amendment July 11 2024. House FY2025 NDAA contained no UAP provisions whatsoever. Final conference outcome again excluded substantive Disclosure Act language. Final NDAA passed House Dec 11 2024 (281-140), Senate Dec 18 2024 (85-14).

FY2026 NDAA (current, 2025-2026) — pending

Sen. Schumer filed UAPDA again July 29 2025 as amendment to NDAA FY2026. Status pending. The current draft includes a narrower requirement that AARO brief Congress on the "number, location, and nature" of UAP intercepts by USNORTHCOM/NORAD. The substantive Disclosure Act framework is again under threat.

What HPSCI Is — And Why The Chair Matters

The House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence (HPSCI) is the U.S. House of Representatives' oversight committee for the U.S. Intelligence Community — CIA, NSA, DIA, NRO, NGA, the FBI's national-security side, and the intelligence components of the military services. It was established in 1977 in the wake of the Church Committee investigations.

What HPSCI does (and why UAP disclosure runs through it)

  • Authorizes the classified intelligence budget (the National Intelligence Program, ~$70-100B/year, almost all of which is itself classified). UAP investigation funding sits inside this pipe.
  • Receives all sensitive compartmented information (SCI) briefings from intelligence agencies on behalf of the House. Members and limited cleared staff are among the only people in Congress who can be briefed on Special Access Programs (SAPs) and Unacknowledged SAPs (USAPs) — the compartmented categories that whistleblowers (Grusch, Nell, Elizondo) allege the UAP material is held inside.
  • Drafts the annual Intelligence Authorization Act (IAA), which sits parallel to the NDAA. The IAA can contain UAP-specific provisions independent of NDAA — and HPSCI controls what goes in.
  • Conducts whistleblower intake for intelligence-community whistleblowers, including the formal Intelligence Community Whistleblower Protection Act process. David Grusch's original 2022 protected disclosure went through HPSCI before becoming public.
  • Holds the gatekeeping authority on whether and when classified UAP material can be moved to the National Archives, declassified, or released under the Disclosure Act framework.

Why the chair specifically matters

The HPSCI Chair has unilateral authority over: hearing schedules, which witnesses get subpoenaed (or not), which staff get TS/SCI clearances, what classified briefings are read in to the full committee, what conference-committee positions HPSCI takes on NDAA UAP amendments, and which whistleblower complaints get advanced versus stonewalled. Functionally, the Chair is a single-person veto point on the entire House-side classified-information disclosure pipeline. This is why Mike Turner's chairmanship 2023-Jan 2025 was disproportionately consequential — he was a one-person bottleneck during the most active UAP-disclosure period in modern Congressional history.

  • Chair: Rep. Rick Crawford (R-AR-1) — Jonesboro/Northeast Arkansas. Appointed January 16, 2025 by Speaker Johnson. Per Rep. Burlison, more disclosure-friendly than Turner.
  • Ranking Member: Rep. Jim Himes (D-CT-4) — Greenwich/Stamford. Long-time committee member. Has been skeptical of some maximalist UAP-disclosure claims but supportive of formal investigation processes.
  • Other members include Reps. Mike Turner (OH-10 — retained committee seat after losing chair), Trent Kelly (MS-1), Darin LaHood (IL-16), Brian Fitzpatrick (PA-1), Austin Scott (GA-8), Ronny Jackson (TX-13), Claudia Tenney (NY-24), and on the minority side Reps. Joaquin Castro (TX-20), Raja Krishnamoorthi (IL-8), André Carson (IN-7), Jason Crow (CO-6), Stacey Plaskett (VI), Sara Jacobs (CA-51), Ami Bera (CA-6), and several others.

Note: HPSCI membership is one of the most coveted assignments in the House because of the access to classified material. Speakers historically reserve the appointments for senior members of their caucus from defense-economy or intelligence-economy districts. The pattern self-reinforces: members chosen because of constituent defense interests then make decisions about disclosure of programs that affect those same constituent interests.

What This Means — The OH-10 Conflict-of-Interest Pattern

The structural picture is stark when you lay out the facts plainly:

  • Wright-Patterson AFB is the alleged keystone site for US UFO secrecy in seven decades of UFO literature and folklore: Hangar 18 / Building 18A, Foreign Technology Division (now NASIC), Project Blue Book HQ 1952-1969, claimed Roswell debris storage, and the named target of David Grusch's 2023 sworn testimony references to compartmented programs.
  • WPAFB is the single largest economic engine in OH-10 — approximately 30,000 military, civilian, and contractor jobs; the largest single-site employer in Ohio; estimated $5+ billion annual regional economic impact. Lockheed Martin, BAE Systems, Raytheon, L3Harris, and General Atomics all maintain significant on-base or adjacent presences.
  • The defense contractors that dominate WPAFB economics are also among the largest historical sources of campaign contributions and PAC funding to Turner specifically — and to the House Armed Services and Intelligence committees more broadly. Turner has been one of the top House recipients of defense-industry PAC money for two decades.
  • Turner held the HPSCI gavel during the FY2024 NDAA conference that decided whether Congress would force presumptive declassification of UAP records — records that, if any of the Grusch / Nell / Elizondo / Mellon claims are even partly correct, would directly implicate programs run out of WPAFB.
  • The institutional incentive vector points one direction. A representative whose district economy depends on classified Air Force programs, whose campaign funding comes from the contractors running those programs, and who personally holds the committee gavel that controls disclosure, has every structural reason to oppose disclosure — and zero structural reason to support it. The fact that he led the gutting is what the incentive structure predicts, not what whistleblowers or Senate sponsors hope for.

The implication is uncomfortable but worth stating directly: the U.S. House intelligence-disclosure pipeline was, by deliberate committee assignment, routed through the representative of the district where the alleged secrets are physically stored. Even with Turner no longer Chair, the larger pattern remains: HPSCI and HASC chairmanships have for decades disproportionately gone to members from defense-economy districts (AL-3, MS, VA, OH-10, etc.), and the structural conflict survives any individual personnel change. Disclosure advocates who treat the gutting as a Turner-specific problem will likely watch the same dynamic repeat with whoever next holds the gavel — unless committee-assignment rules or the underlying district-economic dependencies change.

Government UAP Research Programs

Countries with formal institutional UAP investigation, in approximate order of current activity:

United States — AARO + predecessors

All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) at DoD, 2022–present. Successor to UAPTF (2020-2022), AATIP (2007-2012, Bigelow-contractor era), and AAWSAP (2008-2012, $22M DIA program, produced 38 DIRDs). Trump's February 2026 PURSUE executive order directed broad declassification. The Pentagon released 162 historical UAP files on May 8, 2026 — included files cited in the Iraq, Syria, UAE, Yemen, and Iran cases on this map.

France — GEIPAN (CNES)

Groupe d'Études et d'Information sur les Phénomènes Aérospatiaux Non Identifiés. Toulouse, 1977–present (succeeded GEPAN and SEPRA). Receives ~800 reports per year. Public case-file database. Most institutionally durable Western European UAP program. Investigated Trans-en-Provence 1981 (on this map) and Valensole 1965.

Chile — CEFAA → SEFAA (DGAC)

Comité de Estudios de Fenómenos Aéreos Anómalos, established 1997 by DGAC (Civil Aeronautics Directorate). Renamed Sección de Estudios (SEFAA) in 2021. Receives roughly one case per day. Investigated the Chilean Navy 2014 case on this map.

Uruguay — CRIDOVNI

Comisión Receptora e Investigadora de Denuncias sobre Objetos Voladores No Identificados, within Fuerza Aérea Uruguaya. Founded 1979 — the oldest continuously-operating government UAP investigation office in the world.

Peru — OIFAA (FAP)

Oficina de Investigación de Fenómenos Aéreos Anómalos within Fuerza Aérea del Perú. Active institutional engagement, investigated Chulucanas 2001 (on this map). Cmdr. Julio Chamorro is the public face.

Brazil — FAB declassifications

No permanent UAP office, but the Força Aérea Brasileira has conducted multiple formal investigations: Operação Prato (Pará/Amazon, 1977-78, on this map) and the May 1986 "Noite Oficial dos OVNIs" multi-fighter response (on this map). FAB declassified its UFO files via the Brazilian National Archives in 2009-2015.

Norway — Project Hessdalen

Continuous instrumented scientific study since 1983/84, run jointly by Østfold University College and Italian universities. The longest scientifically-instrumented UAP investigation anywhere. Hessdalen Valley is on this map as a hotspot.

Spain — Spanish Air Force declassification

Ejército del Aire declassified ~1,900 pages of UAP files in 1992-1999 — one of the only major Western air forces to formally release its UFO archive. Files include named-pilot reports and EVA (Aerial Vigilance Squadron) sightings.

Russia (formerly USSR) — Setka MO + Setka AN

Soviet Ministry of Defence (Setka MO) and Academy of Sciences (Setka AN) parallel UAP programs, 1978-1990. Substantially declassified post-1991. Most thorough Soviet-era institutional investigation; Molebka M-Triangle (on this map) was one of its primary investigation sites. Russian Navy declassified additional records in 2009 (Adm. Smirnov section).

Mexico — SEDENA disclosures

Secretaría de la Defensa Nacional. No permanent office but multiple official releases including 2022 thermal video footage of unidentified objects (Mexican Defense Secretary acknowledged on-record). Popocatépetl (on this map) is monitored by CENAPRED webcams.

Australia — RAAF UAP files

Royal Australian Air Force ran UAP investigations through 1960s-90s, files now held by National Archives of Australia. No current dedicated office. Westall 1966 and Valentich 1978 (both on this map) were investigated.

United Kingdom — MoD UFO Desk (closed)

Operated 1950-2009. Files released to the National Archives in batches 2008-2017 (~10,000 pages). Now formally inactive — UK Ministry of Defence position is that the program produced no national-security-relevant findings.

Honorable mentions with thinner institutional engagement: Argentina (CEFAE within FAA), Ecuador (CEIFO), Italy (Aeronautica Militare UAP cell, closed 2016), Canada (Project Magnet 1950-54 only).

Pattern Analysis — What 128 Cases Show

Reading note — selection bias is real

This map is biased toward cases where investigation actually happened, witnesses were credible, and records survived. Every pattern below is filtered through that lens. Findings are flagged where they likely represent real signal vs. where they are more likely artifacts of who reports and who investigates.

1. The map is a snapshot of state capacity, not phenomenon density

Six countries account for ~60% of cases: United States, Russia/USSR, Brazil, France, Chile/Argentina, and the UK. These are exactly the countries with active or historical formal government UAP investigation programs. The 130+ countries with no bar-clearing case are not places where UAP don't happen — they're places without state capacity to investigate or preserve records. The correlation between national-program existence and case-on-map probability is near 1.0.

If you want the map to look different, the lever isn't more witnesses — it's more institutional investigation.

2. Witness class — military observers dominate

Of the 91 single-event cases:

  • ~45 cases have named military pilot or military radar operator witnesses
  • ~15 cases have commercial airline pilot witnesses
  • ~10 cases have police / civilian-government-employee witnesses
  • ~20 cases have civilian-only witnesses

Cases that lack any military or trained observer almost never clear the bar regardless of how dramatic they are. Aguadilla, Yemen, even Skinwalker — they got in only because institutions eventually engaged. We are systematically blind to whatever UAP phenomenon, if any, occurs in spaces civilians frequent but trained observers don't.

3. Temporal patterns

Decadal density is roughly stable from the 1950s onward, with slight upticks in the 1970s-80s and again in the 2010s-2020s. This is misleading — older cases have had decades to surface in declassification, recent cases are still being released. True distribution is likely flatter than it appears.

The dataset is heavily skewed toward nighttime — roughly 75% of cases with a specifiable time occurred between dusk and dawn. Partly real (luminous objects more visible at night, air defense exercises run at night) and partly artifact (atmospheric optical phenomena are also more dramatic at night).

Wave peaks tend to start in late summer / autumn (Belgian wave Nov 1989, Hudson Valley peak 1983-86 spring/summer, Petrozavodsk Sept 1977). Simplest explanation: summer-night sky-watching exposure + atmospheric inversion conditions — not a phenomenon seasonal preference.

4. The nuclear correlation is real and persistent

One of the strongest patterns in the dataset. Cases with explicit nuclear involvement:

  • Malmstrom AFB 1967 — 10 Minuteman ICBMs offline coincident with UAP
  • Iran nuclear facility cluster 2004-2012 — F-14 intercepts at Bushehr, Arak, Natanz
  • Usove (Ukraine) 1982 — UAP-coincident missile-launch-control activation
  • Jaslovské Bohunice 2005 — Slovak nuclear plant incursion + AF response
  • Russell/Baku 1955 — Senator + CIA OSI during Soviet nuclear weapons development
  • Lake Baikal — Soviet Northern Fleet nuclear submarine operations
  • Bikini/Marshall Islands 1946-58 — nuclear test era reports

The correlation repeats across multiple adversarial nations (US + USSR + Iran + Slovakia + UK) and multiple decades. Skeptical reading: nuclear sites have the densest air defense radar, most trained observers, most aggressive interception doctrine — they would generate more documentation regardless of phenomenon. Non-skeptical reading: too clean across adversarial systems to be pure observer bias. Honest assessment: the correlation is real; the causal interpretation is genuinely contested. Selection bias carries it ~70% of the way; the residual 30% is what makes investigators take this pattern seriously.

5. Geography — five terrain types account for nearly all hotspots

  • Active volcanoes — Popocatépetl, Mt Etna. Webcam infrastructure does the selection.
  • Open ocean Navy operating areas — SoCal Op Area, Bass Strait, VACAPES (Graves), Yemen coast, Aguadilla. Sensor-dense by design.
  • High-altitude deserts with extreme dark skies — San Luis Valley, Marfa, Tatacoa, Salar de Uyuni, Zona del Silencio. Maximizes visibility of routine + anomalous.
  • Mountain ranges with pre-Columbian / indigenous sacred status — Capilla del Monte, Mt Shasta, Sedona, Tepoztlán, Peña de Juaica, Hessdalen, Kongka La. Overlap is striking. Probably reflects (a) inherited cultural attention producing sustained witness pools, and (b) the human tendency to mark out specific peaks as sacred is itself partly driven by atmospheric optical features (lenticular clouds, electrostatic discharge) that pre-modern populations interpreted spiritually.
  • Strategic military airspace — Bahía Blanca, Bulawayo, Kayseri-Erkilet AFB, Yakima Reservation (downwind of YTC), nuclear-site cases.

The map has very few cases over genuinely uninhabited terrain — open ocean away from Navy Op Areas, Sahara interior, Tibetan plateau, deep Amazon. Almost certainly observer-density artifact, not phenomenon absence.

6. Morphology — six recurring shapes across 90 years

  • Disc / saucer — dominant pre-1990s (Trans-en-Provence, Father Gill, Westall, Cangzhou, Tully)
  • Cigar / tic-tac / oblong — Nimitz 2004, JAL 1628, Mozambique DETA, Bulawayo 1985, Iran 2012, Bass Strait, Marfa
  • Triangle / boomerang / V-shape — Hudson Valley, Belgian wave, Phoenix Lights, Stephenville, Bonnybridge
  • Orb / sphere — Mosul Orb, Yemen Hellfire, Genkai, Aguadilla, Yakima, Min Min, Brown Mountain, Hessdalen typical
  • Egg / dome — Knowles 1988, Chu Lai 1969, Capilla del Monte, Trans-en-Provence
  • Complex / domed-disc with windows or lights — Father Gill, Lemos Ferreira, Iino-machi reports

The two morphologies that have gained share in the past 20 years are orbs and tic-tacs. Disc reports have declined since the 1990s. Consistent with the hypothesis that morphology reporting is partly culturally shaped — disc reports peaked when "flying saucer" was the dominant cultural frame, and have declined as the Pentagon Tic Tac + orb releases shifted the template.

7. Behavioral patterns — what objects consistently do

  • Pacing aircraft — Mozambique DETA, JAL 1628, Bulawayo, Tehran 1976, Pan Adria 1977, Manises 1979. Unusually consistent across cultures and decades.
  • Sudden near-vertical acceleration / right-angle maneuvers — Nimitz (Mach 5+ in seconds), Belgian wave (40+G), Iran Arak F-14, JAL 1628, Chile Navy 2014. Independently confirmed in radar logs that are difficult to forge.
  • Sensor / electrical interference at close range — Trans-en-Provence vegetation analysis, Iran 2012 F-14 instrument failures, Tehran 1976 F-4 failures, Kuwait 1978 KOC pumping shutdown, Bulawayo 1985, Lake Baikal compass deviations. One of the most repeatable phenomenological reports.
  • Air-to-water transitions — Aguadilla, Lake Baikal "USOs", Bass Strait alleged sea entries. Less robust but cross-cultural.
  • Light emission changes at landing or hover — Trans-en-Provence, Father Gill, Cangzhou, Tully nest formation.
  • Responsive behavior — ECETI / Skinwalker / Mt Adams reports. Most contested, most contaminated by experimenter expectation. Less reliable than the others.

Pacing is engagement — and that is significant

An object that could fly anywhere — given the documented performance envelopes (Mach 5+ accelerations, sustained hypersonic flight, instantaneous direction changes) — and that chooses to fly alongside a specific manned aircraft is making a deliberate engagement choice. Across the strongest cases:

  • Sustained proximity to specific aircraft — Nimitz tic-tac matched the F/A-18's vector; JAL 1628 stayed with the Boeing 747 for 50+ minutes across 400 km; Aer Lingus paced an A320 across two ATC zones; RB-47 1957 was followed across multiple US states
  • Matched maneuvering — pilot accounts describe objects mirroring turns or accelerating in apparent response to heading changes (Lakenheath 1956 RAF Venom pursuit being the textbook case)
  • Reaction to radar lock — Belgian F-16 wave 1989-90: when APG-66 locked on, objects accelerated evasively; when lock broke, behavior resumed. Differential response to sensor state implies awareness of being targeted
  • Withdrawal when actively pursued — Tehran F-4 1976, Lakenheath, multiple others. Position maintained when observed; retreat when intercepted
  • Selective visual avoidance — Nimitz "cloaking" behavior, Andersen Guam 1966 "always in cloud" pattern. Permits radar/IR detection while avoiding clean visual acquisition

These behaviors are most parsimoniously explained by some form of intelligence-controlled platform. Natural phenomena do not selectively pace specific aircraft and respond to radar lock-on. Ball lightning does not track an F/A-18. Plasma vortices do not get behind a chasing Venom and match its turns. The pacing pattern is one of the strongest arguments against natural-phenomenon explanations for the residual category.

What essentially never appears in the dataset

  • Communicative engagement — no linguistic dialogue, no response to deliberate human signaling (lights, radio, prearranged signals), no information transfer about origin or intent
  • Repeatable on-demand summoning — Bledsoe and Elizondo testimony aside as outliers, no instrumented multi-sensor case where humans successfully called the phenomenon to a location under controlled conditions
  • Sustained ground interactions in populated areas with multiple instrumented witnesses — the documented landings (Trans-en-Provence, Father Gill landing approach) are brief and ambiguous
  • Coherent agenda or pattern of contact — no return-on-schedule, no escalation toward dialogue, no cumulative information delivery

The more accurate framing: the phenomenon engages behaviorally but not communicatively. It watches, paces, reacts to sensor state, and withdraws when pursued — all of which require awareness. It does not, however, respond to attempts at deliberate human contact, establish dialogue, or operate on a schedule humans can predict. It engages on its own terms, in the form a surveiller would use rather than the form a contactor would use.

8. Resolution rates

Of the 91 single-event cases:

  • ~12 formally resolved as mundane (Florence chaff, Campeche flares, Greifswald flares, Minsk SLBM, Mogul alloy, Aztec hoax, Lazar, Kalahari hoax, Aguadilla sky lanterns, Mosul Orb partial)
  • ~3 documented disinformation / hoax
  • ~25 contested (multi-sensor data exists with credible prosaic explanations)
  • ~25 testimony-strong, no physical evidence
  • ~25-30 multi-sensor anomalous and unresolved (Nimitz, Belgian, Aguadilla pre-AARO, JAL 1628, Manises, USS Roosevelt FLIR, Cangzhou)

Headline: roughly one-third of bar-clearing cases remain unresolved-anomalous despite decades of investigation. This is not the same as "one-third are real" — it means the existing investigative apparatus has been unable to produce a confident prosaic explanation for ~30 cases. AARO has resolved cases that previous eras left open, suggesting the unresolved fraction would shrink with more investigation, not stay constant.

The cases least likely to ever be resolved: Nimitz 2004, Belgian wave 1989-90, JAL 1628 1986. These are the cases where prosaic hypotheses have been tested most rigorously and have most consistently failed to account for the full record.

9. Patterns that fail to appear in the data

Almost no fine-scale correlation with population density

Within countries, cases do not cluster proportional to population. They cluster proportional to military presence, dark skies, atmospheric conditions, and pre-existing reporting infrastructure. The "happens near people" intuition is mostly a world-map projection artifact — at the country level, population matters; at the within-country level, it matters less.

No clear weather correlation

A clear-sky bias would be expected, but cases appear across all weather types including overcast and rain (Bulawayo 1985, Hessdalen, several Hudson Valley reports).

No sharp pre-1947 → post-1947 step function

Min Min lights (1918), Ghost Rockets (1946), Brown Mountain Lights (1771), Marfa Lights (1883) sit within the same broad phenomenological space as post-1947 cases. The "modern UFO era" beginning in 1947 is more about media history than phenomenon history.

10. Hotspots and bar-clearing cases are geographically disjoint

The 32 hotspot clusters (pink ring pins) and the strongest individual cases live in almost completely different places. Stephenville TX and Hudson Valley NY are the only meaningful crossovers. Everywhere else, the two distributions barely touch. This is not noise — it is a structural feature of the two reporting regimes.

What hotspots have in common:

  1. Remote, rural, low population density. Median population within 25 km is in the low thousands. Marfa, Wycliffe Well, Yakima Reservation, Hessdalen, Iino-machi, Brown Mountain, Min Min, San Luis Valley — all rural.
  2. Dark skies and high horizon visibility. Desert, valley, plateau, or coastal. The sky is observable for hours and night-time clarity is high.
  3. A local researcher or documentation infrastructure on the ground. David Akers logged Yakima for 25+ years. Erling Strand built Project Hessdalen. James Bunnell instrumented Marfa. Without an on-site chronicler collecting reports, the same place produces zero hotspot signal even if events happen there. This is probably the single biggest hotspot-determining factor.
  4. Pre-existing folklore or spirit tradition. Native American sacred-mountain reservations (Yakima, Mt. Adams area), Aboriginal Dreamtime country (Wycliffe Well, Min Min), Appalachian "haint" tradition (Brown Mountain), Sami/Norse spirit-traditions (Hessdalen environs). The cultural lens is open — people will report something unusual rather than write it off.
  5. Often: a local economy that benefits from the reputation. Marfa, Wycliffe Well, Roswell, Hessdalen all monetize visitors. This produces feedback loops — more visitors looking up, more reports.
  6. In a subset: geological or atmospheric mechanisms for repeat optical artifacts. Hessdalen sits over scheelite/zinc-rich rock with elevated radon (possible piezoelectric plasma). Min Min and Brown Mountain are textbook candidates for atmospheric ducting / Fata Morgana / temperature-inversion mirages. Marfa lights have been demonstrated to track car headlights on Highway 67. Hotspot status does not require a real anomalous phenomenon — a recurring optical artifact plus a local chronicler is sufficient.

Where the strongest bar-clearing cases live instead:

  • Active military exercise areas with multiple sensor platforms (Nimitz off Catalina, Roosevelt off VACAPES, RB-47 Gulf Coast, Lakenheath/Bentwaters, Andersen Guam)
  • Commercial aviation corridors with ATC radar (JAL 1628 Alaska, Aer Lingus over Ireland, TACA 510 Guatemala)
  • Nuclear infrastructure with security-cleared observers (Malmstrom missile silos, Indian Springs, Soviet Strategic Rocket Forces sites)
  • Foreign government investigation regimes with declassification pipelines (Chile CEFAA, France GEPAN/COMETA, Belgian F-16 wave, Iran F-4 1976)

Why the two populations are disjoint: hotspots filter for repeat witnessing by attentive ground observers. Bar-clearing cases filter for instrumented sensing by military or commercial platforms. The two filters select for almost opposite geographies — rural+dark+chronicler versus militarized+sensor-dense+declassifiable.

What this means about the phenomenon (cannot be determined from current data):

  • Reading A: The phenomenon is roughly uniformly distributed. Hotspots are places where mundane atmospheric/optical artifacts produce repeated misidentifications by attentive local observers. Bar-clearing cases occur wherever a real anomaly happens to intersect a sensor envelope. The disjointness is fully explained by selection bias, with no claim about phenomenon geography.
  • Reading B: The phenomenon clusters near strategic and military infrastructure. Bar-clearing cases are signal. Hotspots are mostly noise produced by geology, culture, and chronicler effects. The disjointness reflects two different populations: a real one tied to sensitive infrastructure, and a folkloric/atmospheric one tied to rural reporting.

Confidence on the disjointness pattern itself: high. Confidence on which reading is correct: low. Both fit the current data. The honest analytical move is to treat hotspots and bar-clearing cases as different evidentiary classes rather than as different intensities of the same phenomenon. They probably are not measuring the same thing.

11. Hotspots cluster on pre-modern sites of recurring sky-light tradition

Cross-checking all 32 hotspots against documented pre-modern (typically pre-colonial) oral or textual traditions describing sky-light or sky-being phenomena at that specific location produces a striking result: roughly 47% of hotspots have substantial pre-colonial Indigenous traditions tied to the site, and 75% have at least some traditional cultural context. Only 25% are predominantly modern phenomena with no documented deep tradition.

This is a stronger predictor of hotspot status than geology, nuclear proximity, atmospheric mechanism, or any other physical-environment factor on the map.

Tier 1 — Explicit pre-modern tradition tying recurring sky-light phenomena to this exact location

  • Min Min, QLD — Aboriginal Dreamtime describes the lights directly as ancestral spirits. Oral tradition predates the 1918 European written account.
  • Brown Mountain, NC — Cherokee narrative tradition: spirits of women searching for warriors lost in pre-colonial battle. First European written account 1771; Cherokee tradition older.
  • San Luis Valley, CO — Navajo tradition of "sky katchinas (fireballs)" as warnings; 12 tribes used the valley as sacred vision-quest area; Sipapu place-of-emergence cosmology.
  • Kongka La Pass / Ladakh — Ladakhi and Tibetan oral tradition of strange lights predates modernity; broader regional Sanskrit vimana texts (Mahabharata, 10th-century Samarangana Sutradhara) describe sky chariots.
  • Mount Etna, Sicily — Greek mythology of Hephaestus's forge, Roman Vulcan; 2,500+ years of written record describing the mountain's fires and lights.
  • Popocatépetl, Mexico — Aztec/Nahua mythology where the volcano is the warrior Popoca; explicit pre-Hispanic narrative of fires and apparitions in surviving codices.

Tier 2 — Pre-colonial sacred site with sky-being or spirit tradition (less explicitly tied to recurring lights)

Wycliffe Well (Warumungu Dreamtime; Devil's Marbles 100km south), Yakama Reservation (Yakama Nation sky-being tradition; Mt. Adams sacred), Skinwalker Ranch (Ute "Path of the Skinwalker"; Navajo yee naaldlooshii), Cerro Uritorco (Comechingón sacred mountain), Peña de Juaica (Muisca), Lake Baikal (Buryat shamanism), Tepoztlán (Nahua / Tepoztécatl), Tully (Djiru/Mamu/Gulngay Bagjimbiri creator beings), Montserrat (pre-Christian Iberian + Black Madonna apparitions). Nine hotspots.

Tier 3 — Generic or post-colonial traditional overlay

Hudson Valley (Lenape + Sleepy Hollow folklore), Chestnut Ridge (Monongahela + Scots-Irish haint), Marfa (Apache "fallen stars" tradition), Iino-machi (generic Shinto/Buddhist), Bass Strait (Yuin), Bolsón de Mapimí (Yaqui/Tepehuán), Salar de Uyuni (Aymara/Quechua), Tatacoa (Páez), Malargüe (Mapuche). Nine more.

Tier 4 — Predominantly modern, no documented deep tradition

Hessdalen (1981), Bonnybridge (1992), Karamay (1976), Alto Paraíso (1970s), Vale do Paraíba (1957), Bahía Blanca (1962 naval base), Molebka (1980s), SoCal Pacific (1942 open ocean). Eight hotspots — exactly the ones that needed a modern researcher (Erling Strand at Hessdalen) or institutional chronicler to substitute for what cultural tradition provides at Tier 1 sites.

What this means — and three serious confounds

The obvious read is that the phenomenon is real and has been observed continuously for centuries, with Indigenous traditions preserving the observations. That reading is consistent with the data but has three confounds that block confident inference:

  1. Confirmation bias. Indigenous traditions are rich and detailed. Finding "sky beings" or "spirits" in any tradition is easy. For Tier 1 cases the linkage is direct — the tradition specifically describes recurring local light phenomena. For Tier 2 and especially Tier 3, the linkage is weaker and modern researchers may be projecting.
  2. Researcher narrative bias. The "Indigenous people knew about UFOs first" trope is heavily selected for by 20th-century UFO researchers. Cases where Indigenous traditions don't match modern UAP narratives don't get books written about them. Publication bias is substantial.
  3. Reverse causation. Places with oral traditions of sky-spirits become places where modern reports get filed because the local culture has a category for them. The tradition enables hotspot status by enabling reporting, not by indicating phenomenon density. This is the strongest competing explanation.

Confidence: Pre-modern tradition substantially overlaps with hotspot locations — high. Tradition is a stronger predictor of hotspot status than physical-environment factors — moderate-to-high. The tradition reflects a continuous real recurring phenomenon vs. a cultural framework that enables reporting — undetermined.

Tying to section 10 (Reading A vs Reading B):

  • Reading A gains support: Tier 4 hotspots (Hessdalen, Bonnybridge) needed a modern researcher or local UFO group to substitute for what cultural tradition does in Tier 1 places. Take away the cultural framework — or the modern substitute — and these locations look like everywhere else. The mechanism for hotspot status is the interpretive lens, not the underlying phenomenon.
  • Reading B also gains support: six geographically and culturally unrelated traditions — Aboriginal Dreamtime, Cherokee narrative, Navajo sky katchinas, Tibetan vimana, Greek Hephaestus, Aztec Popoca — independently describing recurring sky phenomena at locations that became modern hotspots is not trivially explained by independent cultural invention. Some shared substrate is plausible.

The Tier 1 cross-cultural convergence on the same sites is the single most interesting cross-cultural pattern in the dataset. It does not resolve the Reading A / Reading B question — but it sharpens what the question is. Whatever hotspots are, they are not a 20th-century phenomenon, and any complete explanation must account for the cross-cultural depth.

12. The classified-sensor blackout

The most sensor-rich military installations on Earth produce almost no public UAP record. That absence is itself a data point — arguably one of the most important ones on the map.

Sites where the sensor density is extreme and the public UAP record is near-zero:

  • Kwajalein Atoll / Reagan Test Site (Marshall Islands) — ALTAIR, TRADEX, MMW, ALCOR radars; tuned to track ICBM reentry vehicles and geosynchronous satellites. Zero public UAP cases.
  • Diego Garcia (UK/US Indian Ocean) — Major SIGINT, naval, and strategic airfield. Zero public UAP cases.
  • Pine Gap (Australia) — Primary US-Australia satellite ground station; massive RF coverage. No public UAP cases.
  • Menwith Hill (UK) — Largest NSA SIGINT station outside the United States. Zero public UAP cases.
  • Ascension Island (UK/US South Atlantic) — Range tracking, comms relay, satellite ground station. Zero public UAP cases.
  • PAVE PAWS sites (Beale CA, Cape Cod MA, Clear AK) — Phased-array strategic missile warning radars covering most of US airspace. Almost zero public UAP returns.
  • BMEWS sites (Pituffik Greenland, Fylingdales UK, Clear AK) — Ballistic missile early warning radars. The 1952 Thule case on this map predates BMEWS by nine years; post-1961 BMEWS-era public UAP record is essentially blank.
  • Nevada Test and Training Range (Tonopah, Groom Lake, Nellis) — Classified by design; the public UAP cases that surface (Nellis triangle 1994 gun camera, Edwards 1965 William Coleman) are anomalous in that anything surfaced at all.
  • Eglin AFB / Gulf Range — Largest USAF test range in the continental US. Public UAP record disproportionately thin given the radar coverage.

The strong cases on this map come instead from less-classified contexts: commercial pilots and ATC (JAL 1628, TACA 510, Aer Lingus), Project Blue Book-era reporting when compartmentalization was looser (RB-47 1957, Lakenheath 1956, Andersen Guam 1966), peripheral military encounters at low-classification ranges (Nimitz off SoCal, Roosevelt off VACAPES, Aguadilla DHS), and foreign-government investigations whose declassification rules differ from US ones (CEFAA Chile, COMETA France, GEPAN/SEPRA France, Kohanudnas Indonesia).

There are four ways to read this blackout:

  1. Genuine absence. These sites really don't see UAP. Unlikely given that nearby less-classified sites do — Eglin's range is adjacent to the same Florida airspace that produces the public Aerospace cases, and Kwajalein sits in a region with documented Pacific theater encounters (Andersen Guam 1966, Father Gill 1959 PNG). Confidence: low.
  2. Classification compartmentalization. These sites' data are SAR/SAP-protected by default. A UAP detection on ALTAIR or BMEWS would land inside a compartment that has no declassification pathway analogous to Blue Book or AARO's open record. The phenomenon, if present, is invisible at the unclassified tier — not invisible in reality. Confidence: high.
  3. Signature filtering. These radars are tuned for specific signatures (RV reentry, satellite orbits, ballistic trajectories). Returns outside the expected signature envelope are typically classified as instrumentation artifacts, propagation anomalies, or clutter and discarded at the operator level before reaching any UAP-relevant analyst. Confidence: moderate.
  4. Active suppression. Grusch and Elizondo testimony asserts UAP material recovery and reverse-engineering programs hidden inside SAP structures. If true, the same compartmentalization apparatus that protects those programs would suppress raw sensor detections at sites of strategic interest. The map cannot adjudicate this claim, but the sensor-blackout pattern is consistent with it. Confidence: cannot be assessed from open record.

The takeaway: the unclassified UAP record is built from the periphery of the sensor network, not the center. Wherever the sensors are best and the secrecy is heaviest, the public sees least. Any honest analysis of geographic patterns has to account for this — what looks like a hot region (US East Coast off VACAPES, Brazilian Amazon, Hessdalen) may be hot partly because it is one of the few high-sensor regions where data does reach the public domain.

13. The crash-retrieval asymmetry

The strongest cases on this map are encounters, not crash retrievals. Among the strength-4 and strength-5 anomalous cases — the evidentiary core of the dataset — only one involves a crash with claimed physical material recovery, and that case has serious skeptical challenges.

The crash + retrieval inventory at strength 4-5

  • Dalnegorsk 1986 (4 / anomalous) — the only one. Soviet Academy of Sciences Far Eastern Division recovered lead spherules, mesh fragments with rare-earth composition, ultra-pure molybdenum, and quartz-fiber mesh with claimed shape-memory properties. However: lead isotope analysis matched the Kholodchensky terrestrial deposit in northern Pribaikalye. Russian Academy of Sciences Pseudoscience Commission considers the case closed (Soviet automated reconnaissance balloon with thermite charge).
  • Roswell 1947 (5 / resolved) — Project Mogul balloon array. AARO Volume I (2024) endorses.
  • Mogul Alloy 2024 (4 / resolved) — material claimed alien, analyzed by AARO and a leading lab. Magnesium-zinc-bismuth alloy, terrestrial.
  • Aztec NM 1948 (4 / disinformation) — Frank Scully's 1950 book. Confirmed hoax.
  • Kalahari 1989 (4 / disinformation) — confessed hoax by James van Greunen.
  • El Taire 1978 (4 / contested) — Bolivian/Argentine border crash with alleged US Project Moon Dust recovery. No surfaced primary documentation.
  • Shag Harbour 1967 (4 / anomalous) — object went into Nova Scotia harbour; Royal Canadian Navy and RCMP investigated; underwater search inconclusive; no retrieval.
  • Cuban MiG-21 1967 (4 / anomalous) — Cuban MiG disintegrated during UFO intercept (NSA intercept). The MiG crashed, not the UFO.
  • David Grusch 2023 (4 / testimony) — under sworn Congressional testimony asserts multi-decade non-human craft and biologics recovery programs. Has not publicly named a specific verifiable crash event with date/location/details independently checkable.

What dominates the strength-5 anomalous tier instead

Nimitz Tic Tac 2004, Belgian F-16 wave 1989-90, Tehran F-4 1976, JAL 1628 1986, RB-47 1957, Lakenheath/Bentwaters 1956, Aguadilla DHS 2013, Andersen Guam 1966, Aer Lingus EI-DAA 2018, Trindade 1958, Tananarive 1954 — every single one is an observation of an object that did not come down. The map's bar-clearing core is built on multi-sensor encounters, not on recovered wreckage.

The asymmetry is significant

Public UAP discourse fixates on crash retrieval — Roswell, Grusch's claims, recent "Moment of Contact" attention to Varginha, the long history of Aztec / Kingman / Cape Girardeau / Trinity NM claims. The actual evidentiary record at the strength-4-5 tier contains almost no verifiable crash retrievals. There is a structural mismatch between what the public expects to see in a high-confidence UAP case set and what the high-confidence record actually contains.

Three readings (cannot be distinguished from current data)

  1. The phenomenon doesn't crash. If it is intelligence-controlled non-human technology with high performance, mechanical failure may be vanishingly rare. Encounters are common; crashes are not. Confidence: cannot assess.
  2. The phenomenon is mundane or doesn't exist as a unified thing. Atmospheric, optical, sensor-artifact, and conventional-platform explanations naturally produce repeatable observations but not retrievable physical material. The absence of crash physical evidence is precisely what you'd predict if the residual category is mostly misclassified prosaic events. Confidence: this reading fits the data well.
  3. Crashes happen but recoveries are classified. Grusch's testimony asserts this. The classified-sensor blackout pattern (section 12) is consistent. The public crash-retrieval record would be near-zero precisely because such recoveries would sit in the highest classification compartments. Unfalsifiable from open record. Confidence: cannot rule out.

The crash-retrieval asymmetry is the single most underappreciated feature of the bar-clearing case set. Anyone who claims "Roswell + Grusch + many decades of secret crash programs" as the foundation of UAP reality should reckon with the fact that the strong evidentiary record — when filtered by the same bar applied to every other case on this map — does not contain a single uncontested crash with verified anomalous material. Whatever the phenomenon is, it is observed in flight, not recovered from the ground.

14. The biggest gap in the country-level dataset

East Asia (excluding China) is dramatically underrepresented. South Korea has Seoul 1976. Japan has JAL 1628, JASDF Sato, Iino-machi, Genkai 2025. Taiwan has the 1996 Matsu/Dongyin bar-near miss. That's it for a region with ~200M people across countries with substantial military aviation and Cold War strategic air defense networks.

Most likely explanations: (a) East Asian UFO research operates in Japanese/Korean/Chinese sources hard to penetrate from English-language search; (b) cultural norms produce less Western-style declassification; (c) China's info-control on Xinjiang and Tibet blocks what may be a substantial body of reports.

If a future round adds many cases, East Asia is where the highest signal-per-search-effort would likely come from.

15. The meta-pattern

What 124 cases tell us, accounting honestly for selection bias:

  1. "UAP" is not one thing. The label aggregates: atmospheric optical effects (Marfa, Brown Mountain, Min Min), space launch / rocket debris (Minsk, Khartoum Zuma, parts of Brazilian Official Night), classified military aerospace (Iran's reconnaissance interest), unidentified balloons of multiple origins (Aguadilla, Mosul possibly), and a residual category of multi-sensor events that don't fit any known terrestrial origin.
  2. The residual is small but stubborn. ~25-30 cases. They cluster in time around nuclear weapons infrastructure, in space around active military exercise areas, and in morphology around tic-tac / orb / fast-mover phenomenology. Not random.
  3. It engages behaviorally but not communicatively. The pacing pattern across Nimitz, JAL 1628, Belgian wave, Lakenheath, RB-47, Aer Lingus, and others is selective behavioral engagement — the objects watch specific aircraft, react to radar lock, withdraw when actively pursued. That requires awareness. What is absent is linguistic dialogue, response to deliberate signaling, on-demand summoning, and any coherent contact agenda. The phenomenon engages in the form a surveiller would use, not in the form a contactor would use. This is consistent with classified terrestrial aerospace (any origin), with a non-human intelligence in surveillance posture, or with multiple distinct things lumped under one label. It is not consistent with natural atmospheric/plasma phenomena, which do not selectively pace aircraft or respond to sensor lock.
  4. Institutional dynamics matter as much as the data itself. The most important multi-sensor cases come from countries with adversarial military relationships, and those records have been selectively classified/declassified for political reasons. We are not seeing the underlying phenomenon directly — we are seeing it through the filter of national security policy.
  5. The calibration cases are the most underrated cases on the map. Minsk 1984 (SLBM), Kalahari 1989 (hoax), Campeche 2004 (oil flares), Greifswald 1990 (SAB flares) tell us what a "textbook UFO case" looks like when fully investigated. Anyone serious about the phenomenon should be more interested in these calibrators than in the dramatic unresolved cases.

The map is a snapshot of what 80 years of selective investigation by selective states with selective records has produced. It is not a snapshot of the phenomenon. Whether the residual anomalous fraction will shrink toward zero as investigation continues, or whether it will stabilize as something that doesn't reduce further, is the question AARO and successor programs are trying to answer.

Potential Conclusions — Three Populations, Not One

"UAP" is a label, not a phenomenon. The cases fall into three populations with different witness profiles, sensor signatures, and candidate explanations. Conclusions depend on separating them first. Lumping them together is the most common analytical mistake in the field.

Craft

Variable size — tic-tacs, triangles, discs, sometimes much larger. Multi-sensor verified (radar + IR + visual). Sustained hypersonic performance, near-instantaneous direction changes. Trans-medium movement — observed entering and exiting water at high speed without apparent deceleration, splash, or structural failure (Aguadilla 2013 DHS thermal, Lake Baikal Russian Navy reports since 1958, SoCal Op Area submerged tic-tac contacts, Bass Strait alleged sea entries). Pace military aircraft, react differentially to radar lock, withdraw when actively pursued. These are the cases that anchor the bar-clearing core of the map.

Examples: Nimitz 2004, Belgian wave 1989-90, Tehran F-4 1976, JAL 1628, RB-47 1957, Lakenheath/Bentwaters 1956, Aer Lingus EI-DAA 2018, Andersen Guam 1966, Roosevelt VACAPES 2014-15, Trindade 1958, Chile Navy 2014.

Likely composition, most to least:

  • Classified US aerospace — most likely the largest share. The US has by far the most mature SAP/USAP infrastructure for hiding programs. The Bennewitz case documents AFOSI deliberately using UAP framing as cover for classified US programs. Most strong cases occur in US-controlled or US-aligned airspace. The Belgian wave's timing matches B-2 first flight. The F-117 development cycle (1978-83) produced delta-wing-shape reports across the southwestern US. US R&D outspends Russia and China combined. AFOSI / classified program protection is the most institutionally precedented explanation.
  • Classified adversary aerospace — smaller share. Russian and Chinese hypersonic platforms, low-observable systems in development. The Iranian F-4 1976 case fits Soviet interest in US reconnaissance assets over Iran. Modern hypersonics (DF-ZF, Avangard) plausibly explain some recent encounters. Less plausible for Cold War cases when adversary tech was insufficient. The 2023 Chinese balloon over Montana reframed how much adversary intrusion the US tolerates.
  • Natural atmospheric or plasma phenomenon — smaller still. Substantially downgraded by the pacing pattern. Natural phenomena do not selectively track aircraft and respond to radar lock-on. Ball lightning doesn't get behind a chasing Venom. Explains some subset where the "object" doesn't actually maneuver intelligently.
  • Genuine residual — small but stubborn. Cases that don't reduce cleanly to any of the above. This is the analytically interesting set. Whatever it is, it's observed in flight, surveils military activity, doesn't crash, doesn't communicate, doesn't arrive on demand.

The pacing-and-radar-lock-response pattern requires intelligence control. The question is which intelligence. The institutional opacity around the topic (classified-sensor blackout, UAPDA gutting, AARO mandate hollowing) is more consistent with protecting classified US programs than with adversary intrusion or NHI — but it doesn't rule the others out.

How could humans build something like this?

The Nimitz tic-tac and similar cases describe behavior that breaks known physical limits in four independent ways:

  • 40-100G maneuvering exceeds material strength. Aerospace composites yield at 20-30G; structural metals at similar levels. No public material survives sustained 40G+ in an aircraft-sized object.
  • Biological occupants do not survive 40G+ under conventional force. Humans lose consciousness at ~9G sustained, peak survival ~15-20G briefly. 40G+ kills a human pilot instantly.
  • Sustained hypersonic acceleration with no exhaust implies energy density beyond any public propulsion system. Chemical, nuclear thermal, and ion propulsion all leave signatures. The Nimitz tic-tac left none.
  • Trans-medium movement is independently impossible under conventional engineering. Water is roughly 830× denser than air. No platform optimized for hypersonic atmospheric flight survives water entry at speed.

These are not engineering problems waiting for stronger materials or better fuel. They are physical limits under known mechanics. If the performance data is real, the craft cannot be operating under publicly-known physics.

The simplest physics-coherent explanation is some form of field manipulation — gravity, inertial mass, or local-spacetime control. A craft that manipulates its own inertial field experiences no g-forces internally regardless of external acceleration. The same field, treated as a medium-displacement mechanism, makes air and water equivalent — both are simply medium to be moved around the craft rather than pushed through. One underlying capability resolves the 40G constraint, the biological-survival constraint, the energy-deficit constraint (no reaction mass, no exhaust), and the trans-medium constraint simultaneously. Four anomalies collapse into one physics requirement.

Could humans have discovered this and concealed it? In principle, yes — and the institutional system was built specifically for this scenario. The Atomic Energy Act of 1946 (amended 1954) was created because the US had been caught off guard by the nuclear-physics breakthrough. Atomic theory had been independently developed across multiple countries by physicists who were not government employees. The US could not legally prevent foreign or domestic scientists from rediscovering and publishing what the Manhattan Project had hidden. The Soviets matched the bomb within four years (1949) partly through espionage but partly through independent capability. Congress's institutional answer was to create a legal category — "Restricted Data" — that automatically classifies covered information at the moment of creation, before any government employee ever sees it, applying even to civilian scientists who independently derive it. The doctrine is forward-looking by design: it exists so that the next revolutionary physics breakthrough cannot escape government control before policy catches up. Court-tested in US v. Progressive (1979). National laboratories (Los Alamos, Sandia, Lawrence Livermore, Oak Ridge, Idaho National Lab) hold Q-clearance researchers conducting born-classified work the public cannot evaluate. Public-key cryptography sat classified at GCHQ for 25 years before Diffie-Hellman and RSA independently rediscovered it — a demonstration that the system can in fact hold breakthroughs for decades. Bob Lazar's 1989 claims about a "gravity-wave amplifier" architecture at S-4 describe exactly the kind of work born-classified doctrine would protect — his credentials are mostly unverified, but the structure of the claim fits.

But the duration is unprecedented if true. Manhattan was concealed three years. Public-key crypto twenty-five. The NRO's existence thirty-one (and only its existence, not its capabilities). 60-80 years of complete operational secrecy for a working gravity-manipulation program has no historical precedent. The longer concealment runs, the more people read in, the more contractor relationships accumulate, the more documentary trail exists. The Manhattan analogue establishes that humans can hide breakthrough physics. It does not establish that humans can hide it for eighty years.

The honest read. The data forces a hard choice. If the Nimitz-class performance numbers are real, the craft operates under physics not in the public catalog. That physics has either been discovered by humans and concealed (precedented mechanism, unprecedented duration) or has not been discovered by humans — which means someone else discovered it. The third option — that the multi-sensor + visual + radar convergence is misreading the actual performance — requires explaining away the convergence itself. No clean exit exists. Whichever direction you pick, you are claiming something significant about physics, secrecy, or the reliability of military sensors.

Orbs

Small, self-luminous, slow-moving or hovering. Appear at residences, ranches, sacred sites. Typically 1-5 witnesses, rarely instrumented. Often religiously or mystically framed by witnesses.

Examples: Bledsoe Cape Fear, Elizondo's home (passive recurrence, not summoned), Skinwalker Ranch, Brown Mountain, Marfa, Hessdalen, Min Min, Yakima Reservation.

Likely composition, most to least:

  • Small classified ISR or SOF drones — especially residential orbs near military infrastructure. Bledsoe's property is adjacent to Fort Bragg (JSOC, USASOC, 75th Rangers — highest US special-operations density on earth). Elizondo's experiences began after his AATIP work. Bingham's "summoning" produces what's identifiable as drones. Less plausible pre-2000.
  • Atmospheric plasma and ionization — earth-light hypothesis (Hessdalen scheelite + radon), Fata Morgana (Min Min), ball lightning, temperature-inversion mirages.
  • Misidentified conventional objects — Venus, satellites, ISS passes, Iridium flares, distant aircraft, fireballs, drones — primed by local tradition or expectation.
  • Cultural-experiential framing — for the contactee subset (Bledsoe, ECETI, etc.), shared experiential template within a tight community is a real candidate.
  • Small residual genuinely unidentified — possibly distinct from Craft, possibly related.

Cross-cultural pre-modern oral traditions describe similar phenomena at many of these locations (Pattern Analysis section 11). Whether that reflects a continuous real phenomenon or a continuous cultural framework that enables reporting is undetermined.

Other

Real physics, misclassified as anomalous when investigation is incomplete. Atmospheric optics, plasma, sensor artifacts, conventional platforms identified after rigorous analysis. The largest category by far across all UAP reports — and a meaningful fraction of even the bar-clearing map.

Resolution paths:

  • Atmospheric mirages and inversions — Min Min (Fata Morgana via Pettigrew), Brown Mountain (temperature inversion), Marfa (Highway 67 headlights, partially demonstrated)
  • Space launch / reentry events — Minsk 1984 (SLBM), Khartoum 2018 (SpaceX Zuma), Brazilian "Official Night" 1986 (partial)
  • Military flares and test munitions — Greifswald 1990 (SAB target flares), Phoenix Lights 1997 (second-phase A-10 flares)
  • Industrial / commercial signatures — Campeche FAM 2004 (Cantarell oil platform flares)
  • Confirmed hoaxes — Kalahari 1989 (van Greunen confession), Aztec 1948 (Scully), Lazar (no credible verification)
  • Reconnaissance balloons — Mosul Orb 2016 (AARO), 2023 Chinese balloon reframed the category entirely
  • Misanalyzed material — Mogul Alloy 2024 (AARO determined terrestrial magnesium-zinc-bismuth alloy)
  • Sensor artifacts — FLIR gimbal rotation (the "Gimbal" video's spin is the camera's gimbal, not the object), radar anomalous propagation under inversion conditions

The calibration cases on the map (Minsk, Kalahari, Campeche, Greifswald, Mogul Alloy) explicitly show what a "textbook UFO case" looks like when fully investigated. Anyone serious about the phenomenon should weight these heavily — they tell you what kinds of strong cases turn out to be when somebody does the work.

Where the categories blur

The categories are not clean. Borderline cases worth flagging:

  • Aguadilla 2013 — orb morphology but Craft-tier performance (air-to-water transition, apparent splitting). The map treats it as Craft because of DHS sensor data.
  • Hessdalen — orb appearance with instrumented research suggesting plasma mechanism. Could be Other that became Orb through chronicler effect (Project Hessdalen since 1983).
  • WWII "foo fighters" — orbs that paced military aircraft. Orb shape with Craft behavior. Either A-population objects with B-population behavior, or natural plasma effects miscategorized.
  • Skinwalker Ranch — multi-modal: orbs, "doorways," instrument malfunction, animal effects. Fits no single category cleanly. May indicate categories are aspects of one phenomenon, or that the Ranch is contaminated by hoaxing and confirmation bias across decades.

Some real overlap exists. Whether it reflects fundamentally different phenomena with occasionally overlapping behaviors, or different aspects of one underlying thing, is undetermined.

Bottom line

Most UAP reports are Other. Take that as the baseline. The calibration cases anchor what the answer looks like when investigation is done properly.

Craft are real and contain intelligence-controlled platforms. The pacing pattern requires this. Most likely a US-dominant classified terrestrial mix, with a smaller adversary share, a smaller still natural-phenomenon share, and a small residual that fits none of those.

Orbs are a real recurring category but probably not what the disclosure community claims. The mix includes small classified drones, atmospheric effects, misidentifications, and cultural-experiential framing more than it includes literal non-human contact tools.

If anything is genuinely non-human, it sits in the small Craft residual. Observed in flight, surveilling military activity, not crashing, not communicating, not arriving on demand. Not the Roswell crash narrative. Not the Bledsoe contactee narrative. Something smaller and stranger than either.

Anyone asserting more certainty than this — in either direction — is overclaiming.
